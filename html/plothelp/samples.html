Timeout overlays and sample grid traces help

Science plots contain diagnostic traces relating the sampling and errors from the instruments.

Timeouts

Dive timeouts and Climb timeouts overlays show the samples that took longer then the instrument specified timeout. The timesout times are taken from the <instrument>_timeouts_times netCDF variable where <instrument> is the insturment name.

Sample stats

The Dive samples stats and Climb samples stats traces show the samples per meter for 5 meter bins. This trace is not tied to a axis - use the hovertips to find the actual values.

Sample grid

The Dive sample grid and Climb sample grid traces graphically show what the commanded sampling grid is. This trace is not tied to a axis - use the hovertips to find the actual values.

The calculation of these traces use the pre-mission selftest and the uploaded science and scicon.sch to generate a history of the commanded sampling and will not work without a pre-launch selftest. Some very old Seaglider firmware versions do not supply enough information in the selftest to allow the sampling data to be calculated.
