WKB Buoyancy Frequency Plot Help

This plot shows buoyancy frequency (N) profiles gridded across a trailing window of dives (the --wkb_dives_back most recent dives, inclusive of the current one), and is the basis for the WKB stretching used to derive the proposed sampling schedule shown in the companion WKB Sampling Schedule plot. Depth increases downward; the screenshot below is from SG272's dive 300.



5-m Buoyancy Frequency

The faint gray, jagged 5-m buoyancy frequency traces are the per-cast (dive/climb) buoyancy frequency profiles, one per cast in the trailing dive window, gridded at 5 m depth spacing - each cast contributes one trace, so a longer --wkb_dives_back window means more (and generally noisier-looking, just from there being more of them overlaid) traces.



Smooth Buoyancy Frequency

The solid black smooth buoyancy frequency trace is the cross-cast mean of the 5-m traces above, smoothed over depth, used as the basis for WKB stretching.



Uniform Buoyancy Frequency

The dashed black vertical uniform buoyancy frequency line is the single reference frequency that WKB-stretched sampling (shown in the companion schedule plot) is made uniform with respect to - the mean of the smooth trace above, from the bottom of the fixed-rate top zone (--wkb_buckets_depths' first value) down to the deepest depth reached.



Options

These options configure the WKB computation shared by this plot and the companion WKB Sampling Schedule plot:

  • --wkb_dives_back: How many dives back (inclusive of the current dive) to include.
  • --wkb_buckets_depths: Comma-separated depth bucket boundaries (m), e.g. 100,200,300,400,500,600,700,800,1200. The first value marks the bottom of the fixed-rate top zone used when computing the uniform reference frequency.
  • --wkb_relative_n: Comma-separated candidate point-count multipliers (relative to the current sampling), e.g. 0.8,1,1.5 - see the companion WKB Sampling Schedule plot.
  • --wkb_dz: Depth bin size (m) used when gridding dives.
  • --wkb_z_max: Maximum depth (m) used when gridding dives.
  • --wkb_top_sampling_rate: Fixed sampling interval (s) imposed above the first depth bucket - see the companion WKB Sampling Schedule plot.
  • --wkb_buoyfreq/--no-wkb_buoyfreq: Whether to generate this plot at all (default on) - the companion WKB Sampling Schedule plot is always generated regardless.
