Pitch Control Plot Help

This plot is used to check/re-derive the pitch trim calibration parameters (C_PITCH, pitch GAIN) by comparing observed vehicle pitch angle against pitch motor (VBD position) control.

  • Observed pitch points are actual pitch angle vs. pitch control counts seen during the dive; points not used in the fit are shown in a different marker (e.g. taken during a transient right after a pitch move).
  • Model as flying on glider is the fit implied by the glider's currently commanded C_PITCH/GAIN/ SHIFT parameters.
  • Linear fit, Nonlinear fit fixed shift, and Nonlinear fit are alternative fits computed from this dive's data, each reporting its implied C_PITCH/ GAIN(/SHIFT) and RMS error (model pitch minus observed pitch, in degrees).

Lower RMS is a better fit; a model trace that tracks the observed points closely across the full pitch-control range indicates good trim. Large, systematic disagreement suggests C_PITCH or GAIN should be updated.
