Mission Energy Plot Help

This plot tracks energy consumption over the course of the mission and projects how many dives/days remain before the configured reserve is reached.

Energy traces (left axis, kJ)

  • Fuel Gauge traces (10V/24V) show energy used as measured directly by the battery pack's fuel gauge hardware, when present.
  • Modeled Use traces show energy used as estimated from logged component on-times and their known power draw (see the per-device breakdown further down the plot, which uses the color key in the code for devices such as VBD_pump, Pitch_motor, Roll_motor, Iridium, etc). This is the only estimate available on packs without a fuel gauge.

Voltage traces (right axis, volts)

Min Volts Observed traces show the minimum pack voltage seen each dive, separately for the low-voltage (LV) and high-voltage (HV) packs where the glider has a split-voltage system.

Mission Days (far-right axis)

Elapsed mission time in days, for correlating energy use against calendar time rather than just dive number.

Endurance estimate

The text under the x-axis title reports the projected dives/days remaining before the configured reserve (mission_energy_reserve_percent) is reached, and an estimated end date. Where a fuel gauge is present, one estimate is shown based on fuel-gauge data and a second based on the modeled-use data; the two can diverge if the model's assumed device power draws are off.
