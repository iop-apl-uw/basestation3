Compass Calibration Plot Help

This plot shows the raw and corrected magnetometer readings (X field vs. Y field) used to fit the truck compass's hard-iron/soft-iron calibration. A well-calibrated compass traces out a circle centered on the origin; distortion (an off-center or elliptical shape) indicates uncorrected magnetic interference.

  • uncorrected is the raw magnetometer reading.
  • hard corrected applies only the hard-iron (offset) correction.
  • corrected (when soft-iron correction is computed) applies both hard- and soft-iron correction.
  • onboard/autocal traces (when present) show the correction computed by the glider's onboard/autonomous calibration routine, for comparison against the basestation's fit.

Each trace's legend label includes its circularity value in parentheses - closer to 0 is a better circular fit. The autocal trace also reports its angular coverage (how much of the full rotation was sampled) and how many points were used in the fit. The title reports the fitted hard-iron offset (and soft-iron matrix, if computed).
